都铎王朝时期（1564—1603）：莎士比亚出生于1564年，正值都铎王朝伊丽莎白一世统治的黄金时代（1558—1603）。这一时期是英国文艺复兴的高峰，伊丽莎白一世推行宗教改革、鼓励文化艺术发展，莎士比亚的早期创作（如《仲夏夜之梦》《威尼斯商人》等）均诞生于这一阶段。都铎王朝的君主专制和民族主义氛围深刻影响了莎士比亚作品中对王权、人性的探讨。

斯图亚特王朝初期（1603—1616）：1603年伊丽莎白一世去世后，苏格兰国王詹姆斯六世（英格兰称詹姆斯一世）继承王位，开启斯图亚特王朝。莎士比亚晚年（1603—1616）的创作如《李尔王》《麦克白》等悲剧，反映了斯图亚特早期社会矛盾激化的背景。尽管王朝更迭，莎士比亚仍受到詹姆斯一世的庇护，其剧团更名为“国王供奉剧团”。

###### 一、源流与发展

1. 意大利起源与彼特拉克的影响

十四行诗（sonnet，son表示歌，net表示小，意为短的歌），起源于13世纪的意大利

西西里王国的宫廷诗人troubadours，最初为抒情小调（sonetto），以彼特拉克的创作为代

表，形成了彼特拉克体（又称意大利体），结构为前八行（octave）和后六行（sestet），

韵式为ABBA ABBA CDE CDE。

2. 传入英国与本土化

16世纪，十四行诗传入英国，托马斯·怀亚特（Thomas Wyatt）等诗人将其引入并改造。莎士比亚在此基础上发展出莎士比亚体，结构为三节四行（quatrains）加两行对句（couplet），韵式为ABAB CDCD EFEF GG，频繁换韵，每行10个音节（五的整数倍），两个音节为一个音部，读起来是后一个音节重读。五步抑扬格Iambic pentameter，penta意为五 Iambic抑扬格 pentameter五音步（five-foot），抑是轻读，扬是重读，抑扬格最后一个音节是扬。

彼得拉克体十四行诗/意大利体十四行诗vs. 莎士比亚体十四行诗/英格兰体十四行诗

3. 文艺复兴与人文主义背景

莎士比亚的十四行诗创作于1590-1598年，正值文艺复兴思潮席卷欧洲。诗中的人文主义精神强调人的价值、美的永恒与爱的力量，反映了对时间、死亡与不朽的深刻思考。

###### 二、形式与结构特点

1. 严格的格律

音步与音节：每行10个音节，采用五音步抑扬格（iambic pentameter），节奏感强烈。

押韵格式：遵循ABAB CDCD EFEF GG的韵式，结尾的对句（couplet）常以警句形式总结全诗，如“只要人类在呼吸，眼睛看得见，我的诗就活着，使你的生命绵延”。

2. 起承转合的叙事逻辑

前三节四行逐步展开主题，最后两行对句实现转折或升华，形成“提出问题-分析问题-结论”的经典结构。

###### 三、主题与思想内涵

1. 爱情与美的颂歌

诗中常将“爱人”与自然之美（如夏日）对比，强调其超越时间的永恒性，如第18首“你永久的夏天决不会凋败”。

2. 时间与死亡的抗争

通过诗歌赋予美与爱以不朽，如“死神不能夸说你在他的阴影里徘徊，若你在不朽的诗中与时间同长”。

3. 人性的复杂与矛盾

部分诗歌涉及对欲望、背叛的反思，如对“黑女士”（Dark Lady）的复杂情感，展现爱与道德的冲突。

###### 四、语言与艺术特色

1. 丰富的修辞手法

比喻与拟人：如将时间拟为“镰刀”，将夏日比作短暂的美。

意象叠加：自然意象（玫瑰、狂风、夏日）与抽象概念（时间、死亡）交织，增强画面感。

2. 警句与哲理性

结尾对句常凝练为格言，如“爱是永不游移的灯塔光”，兼具文学性与哲思。

3. 情感的张力与矛盾

诗中既有对理想化爱情的赞美，亦包含对现实缺憾的哀叹，形成强烈的戏剧性冲突。

###### 五、影响与地位

莎士比亚的154首十四行诗被誉为“十四行诗的巅峰”，其形式与主题对后世诗人（如济慈、勃朗宁）影响深远。中文译本中，梁实秋、屠岸等译者的版本兼具忠实性与文学性，成为经典。

Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day?

Origins of the story

The basic story in which young lovers choose to die together rather than live apart is an ancient one. The Roman poet Ovid’s Metamorphoses, for instance, contains the story of the separated lovers Pyramus and Thisbe, which Shakespeare parodied in his next play, A Midsummer Night’s Dream. In the 3rd century CE, the story of Ephesiaca, by the Greek writer

Xenophon of Ephesus, sees the 16-year-old Habrocomes and 14-year-old Anthia embark on a suicide pact. The story of Romeo and Juliet in particular became popular in 16th-century Italy in versions such as Luigi da Porto’s 1530 novel Giulietta e Romeo. The two feuding families in the story, the Montecchi (Montagues) of Verona and the Capeletti (Capulets) of Cremona, were historical, and the poet Dante writes of them in his Purgatorio as being at the center of civil strife in 13th-century Italy. Shakespeare’s immediate source, though, was an

English version of the story written as a long poem in 1562 by Arthur Brooke entitled The Tragical History of Romeus and Juliet


Many of the principal characters in Romeo and Juliet are much the same as in Brooke’s poem, but Shakespeare’s treatment of the story is very different. In Romeo and Juliet, the lovers have just one night together after their wedding; in Brooke’s poem, Romeo visits Juliet every night for a month or two. Shakespeare’s story is faster, and this serves to heighten the tragic brevity of the romance. Shakespeare has a very different attitude toward the lovers. Brooke

thoroughly disapproves of them; they are“unfortunate lovers, thrilling themselves to unhonest desire, neglecting the authority and advice of parents and friends.” Shakespeare, however, is on their side. In Romeo and Juliet, they are innocent victims—Juliet is just 13—and in the beauty and ardor of their love, they soar far above their parents’ bickering and bitterness. It is this largehearted sympathy that gives Shakespeare such wide appeal, and made his message so subversive at the time. While the characters in Brooke’s poem are largely archetypes, in Romeo and Juliet, they are much more fleshed out and real. Shakespeare also brings minor characters such as the well-intentioned Friar Laurence and the chatterbox Nurse（搞笑的人物更能衬托出悲剧） fully to life. Romeo’s quick-witted and earthy friend Mercutio is entirely Shakespeare’s creation, one of his most appealing. It is Mercutio’s death in a sword fight at the beginning of Act 3 that wrenches the mood of the play dramatically from comedy to tragedy

创作背景：

Romeo and Juliet was written at a time when England was as divided as it has ever been. King Henry VIII had split from the Roman Catholic church in 1533, and the wounds this had caused were still red raw. The Protestant regime of the reigning Queen Elizabeth I, Henry VIII’s daughter, was under attack from Catholics both from within the country and from abroad. The regime was fighting back, led by the queen’s first minister Robert Cecil. Catholic recusants were hunted down ruthlessly by Cecil’s henchmen, while agents of the Catholic Counter Reformation conspired to bring Protestant England back into the fold. The younger generation, of which Shakespeare was one, were indeed caught between the warring factions—and it may be that Romeo and Juliet was intended as a reminder to them of the tragic consequences of their bitter conflict for their offspring. Romeo and Juliet could have found counterparts in England, with Protestants and Catholics replacing the Montagues and Capulets as the feuding neighbors. 家族斗争隐喻宗教之争 火药桶阴谋（Gunpowder Plot）
